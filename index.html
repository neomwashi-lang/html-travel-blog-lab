Fire Island Adventures

My Fire Island Gateaway

My visit to Fire Island National Seashore was a refreshing escape from the usual coastal crowds, thanks largely to the absence of cars on most of the island, which gives the place an unhurried, almost timeless feel as visitors walk wooden boardwalks between dune grass and weathered beach cottages. The undeveloped beaches stretch for miles, backed by rolling dunes that protect a surprisingly varied landscape, and a highlight was wandering through the Sunken Forest, a rare maritime holly forest whose canopy sits below the dune line, creating a cool, shaded contrast to the open shoreline. The historic Fire Island Lighthouse, still standing watch at the island's western end, offers sweeping views of the Atlantic and the Great South Bay for those willing to climb its spiral stairs, while the laid-back hamlets, including the culturally significant communities of Cherry Grove and the Pines, add a distinct social charm to the natural scenery. Reaching the island by ferry only added to the sense of arrival somewhere apart from ordinary life, and between the birdwatching, swimming, and quiet trails, it struck me as a destination that rewards slowing down rather than checking off sights.
